CAP GUIDELINES

Ministry's Framework for Restorative Justice

Prior to accepting any diversion referral:

  • All victims approached to participate in a community accountability program are apprised of support services available in their communities (e.g. VictimLinkBC, a multi-lingual information line for victims available across BC 24 hours a day, 7 days a week at 1-800-563-0808 or www.victimlinkbc.ca)
  • All participants fully understand the process.
  • The requirements and limits of confidentiality are fully explained to all participants.
  • All parties choose to participate in the restorative justice process voluntarily and are not under any threat or coercion to do so by any party.
  • A letter of informed consent is obtained from all parties participating in the process.

Prior to accepting any diversion referral:

  • The nature of the alleged offence has been explained and the offender voluntarily accepts responsibility for the incident.
  • Alleged offenders (and parents or legal guardians) have been fully apprised of their rights to legal counsel and their right to have the matter heard in the courts
  • The alleged offender is fully aware of their responsibility to comply with any agreement reached through the process.
  • All agreements are entered into voluntarily and with the consent of all parties.

The a CAP operates in such a manner that:

  • All persons, offenders and victims, are treated with respect and dignity at all times.
  • Involvement in the process is safe and does not endanger any participants.
  • All program volunteers have criminal records checks.
  • All volunteers are adequately trained to participate in the program.
  • A complaints process is established and accessible to all participants.
  • Appropriate record keeping is undertaken and case file management is maintained.
  • Confidentially of participants is maintained.
