SOCIAL SERVICES GROUP LIABILITY PROGRAM ELIGIBILITY

All programs are encouraged to secure liability insurance for their volunteers and are advised to seek legal advice regarding risks and liabilities associated with implementing and operating a program.

There are several ways that your organization can obtain liability insurance:

  • If your program is part of an umbrella organization, your volunteers may be eligible for coverage under its master policy.
  • If your volunteers report directly to a municipal employee or a municipal contractor, coverage may be available through the municipality’s insurance.
  • If your organization is affiliated with an agency that is under contract to the province, it may already be covered by government’s Social Services Group Liability Program (SSGLP).

It may be in the best interest of programs to seek accident coverage, commonly called Accidental Death and Disability (AD&D), for their volunteers. If this coverage is desired, it is up to the funding recipient to arrange for it through the commercial insurance market.

SOCIAL SERVICES GROUP LIABILITY PROGRAM

The Social Services Group Liability (SSGLP) coverage provides $2,000,000 per occurrence of commercial general liability insurance. The program is similar to a group insurance program in that all members of the group share similar characteristics and can therefore be covered under one policy. The Province provides this coverage to eligible contractors at no cost. If service providers feel this limit is not adequate, they can purchase excess coverage from the commercial insurance market, usually at a reasonable cost.

Community Accountability Programs have been offered coverage through SSGLP to help volunteer-based programs get started. SSGLP is not designed to replace commercial insurance nor to cover interests that should be insured elsewhere, and some programs may have ‘outgrown’ their eligibility for this coverage.

Ineligible service providers:

  • Service provider carries their own commercial general liability insurance
  • Service provider carries their own commercial general liability insurance
  • Registered professionals (e.g., a nurse, a clinical counsellor, a social worker, etc.)

Ineligible contracts/services:

  • Services outside the scope of Province Authority’s service plan, mandate letter, or enabling legislation
  • Professional services
  • Contracts that are exclusively for professional services (e.g., nursing, clinical counselling, social work, etc.) are entirely ineligible
  • Contracts that include incidental professional services in the context of a larger engagement for non-professional services may be enrolled in SSGLP; however, the professional services are excluded from coverage and no professional liability claims will be covered by SSGLP
  • Services are provided directly to the Province Authority (i.e., first party service contracts)
  • Funding-only contracts (i.e., grants: no term, no services provided directly in exchange for funds)

Social Services Group Liability Program Coverage:

Limit of Coverage: $2,000,000 per occurrence

Type of Coverage: Third Party Liability Insurance

Deductible: $1,000 on Third Party Property

Included in the coverage:

  • Bodily Injury Liability
  • Property Damage Liability
  • Personal and Advertising Liability
  • Employees and Volunteers as Additional Insureds when performing the Services
  • Subcontractors as Insureds when performing the Services under a written contract

This coverage only extends to the performance of the Services described in a written agreement between the Province Authority and a service provider where those Services are performed by the service provider and/or by approved subcontractors who have entered into a written agreement to perform the Services. For greater certainty, coverage only pertains to Services described in the main agreement no matter how many times the Services have been transferred to another layer of service providers.

Not included in the coverage:

  • Damage to building or contents and other property owned, rented, used or occupied by the service provider
  • Activities not described in the main agreement with the Province Authority
  • The service provider’s business risks (for example, employment practices liability, directors’ and officers’ liability, errors and omissions liability)
  • Professional liability
